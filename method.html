The method

Five dimensions of AI resilience.

Our diagnostic turns AI adoption from a vague ambition into a scored, repeatable operating model. We look at the business conditions that decide whether AI delivers value or becomes another abandoned pilot.

1

Leadership & readiness

Is the intent real, funded and owned at the top?

2

Data & tooling

Is the data and tech stack fit to build on?

3

Process fit

Where does AI map to real workflows and value?

4

People & skills

Can the team actually use and trust it?

5

Governance & risk

Is it safe, compliant and controlled?

Leadership & readiness

We test whether AI has an accountable sponsor, realistic budget, decision rights and business outcomes attached to it. Without this, AI remains a side project.

Data & tooling

We map the systems, document stores, data quality and security controls that determine whether AI can work safely with the information the business already depends on.

Process fit

We find where work is repetitive, slow, duplicated or knowledge-heavy, then score opportunities by value, feasibility, risk and adoption effort.

People & skills

We assess confidence, practical skills, resistance, review habits and training needs so staff can use AI responsibly rather than secretly or inconsistently.

Governance & risk

We review policy, shadow AI, client data, approval workflows, vendor risk and human review so adoption can scale without creating unmanaged exposure.

Sector examples

The same framework, made specific to the business.

Accountancy practice

Client emails, bookkeeping workflows, tax-return drafting, document handling and GDPR-safe AI usage.

Outcome: fewer admin hours without compromising compliance.

Recruitment agency

Candidate screening, job adverts, interview summaries, CRM workflows and bias-aware governance.

Outcome: more recruiter time spent placing candidates.

Manufacturer

Production reports, maintenance planning, inventory signals, quality documentation and audit trails.

Outcome: better planning and reduced operational drag.

Law firm

Document review, legal research, contract drafting, approval workflows and client-confidentiality controls.

Outcome: more lawyer time spent on judgement and analysis.
